# Competitive Analysis

## Quick Competitor Research

### Data Sources (free)
1. **Website** — Pricing page, feature list, case studies, blog
2. **App Store / G2 / Capterra** — User reviews, ratings, complaints
3. **Job Postings** — Tech stack, team size, growth direction
4. **Social Media** — Engagement, content strategy, community size
5. **BuiltWith / Wappalyzer** — Tech stack detection

## Feature Comparison Matrix

```markdown
| Feature | Us | Competitor A | Competitor B |
|---------|:--:|:-----------:|:-----------:|
| Feature 1 | ✅ | ✅ | ❌ |
| Feature 2 | ✅ | ❌ | ✅ |
| Feature 3 | 🔜 | ✅ | ✅ |
| Pricing (starter) | $X | $Y | $Z |
| Free tier | ✅ | ❌ | ✅ |
```
✅ = has it, ❌ = doesn't, 🔜 = planned

## SWOT Template

| | Helpful | Harmful |
|---|---------|---------|
| **Internal** | **Strengths:** What you do better | **Weaknesses:** Where you fall short |
| **External** | **Opportunities:** Market gaps to exploit | **Threats:** Competitor advantages, market shifts |

## Positioning Statement
```
For [target customer]
who [need/pain point],
[product name] is a [category]
that [key benefit].
Unlike [competitor],
we [key differentiator].
```

## Battle Card (for sales)
```markdown
## vs [Competitor Name]

### When we win:
- [Scenario where we're clearly better]

### When we lose:
- [Scenario where they're stronger]

### Their weaknesses:
- [Common complaints from their users]

### Our response to their strengths:
- [How to counter their advantages]

### Killer question to ask prospects:
- "[Question that highlights our advantage]"
```

## Review Mining
Read competitor's 1-3 star reviews on G2/Capterra/App Store:
- Group complaints into themes
- Each theme = a potential differentiator for you
- Use their users' exact words in your marketing copy
